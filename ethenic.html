Peplum Top with Sharara

Peplum tops with Shararas give quite a look. When worn correctly, it can yield diminutive waistlines and elongated figures. Worn poorly, it can lead to exaggerated hips and unnecessary bulk.A peplum is a flared ruffle sewn into the waistline of a blouse, skirt, jacket, or dress to add extra frill and accentuate the waist. With roots in ancient Greece, the peplum dress rose to widespread popularity in the nineteenth century.Peplum comes from a Greek word for 'tunic' and is an almost skirt-like frill or addition. They were once fitted to a waistcoat or doublet, but now to a woman's bodice, extending it below the waistline.
